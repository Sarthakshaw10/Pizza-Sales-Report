# Pizza-Sales-Report
Analyzed pizza sales data using SQL and Tableau to evaluate business performance and customer behavior. Calculated KPIs such as total revenue, average order value, and total orders, and built dashboards showing hourly trends, category sales, and top products, enabling data-driven insights for sales strategy and menu optimization.
1. Introduction

The objective of this project was to analyze pizza sales data to understand business performance, customer purchasing behavior, and product performance. Using SQL for data analysis and Tableau for visualization, the project aims to generate insights that help improve sales strategy, product offerings, and operational efficiency.

2. Tools & Technologies

MS SQL Server – Data storage and query analysis

SQL – Data extraction and KPI calculations

Tableau – Interactive dashboard visualization

Excel – Data preprocessing

3. Dataset Overview

The dataset contains transactional data related to pizza orders including:

Order ID

Order Date & Time

Pizza Category

Pizza Size

Pizza Name

Quantity Sold

Price

Total records analyzed include:

21,000+ orders

49,000+ pizzas sold

4. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The following KPIs were calculated using SQL queries:

Total Revenue

Total revenue generated from all pizza orders.

Result:
$817.86K

Average Order Value

Average revenue per order.

Formula:

Total Revenue / Total Orders

Result:
$38.3

Total Pizzas Sold

Total quantity of pizzas sold.

Result:
49.6K pizzas

Total Orders

Total number of orders placed.

Result:
21.4K orders

Average Pizzas per Order

Formula:

Total Pizzas Sold / Total Orders

Result:
2.32 pizzas per order

5. Data Analysis & Insights
Hourly Sales Trend

The hourly analysis revealed:

Peak order times:

12 PM – 1 PM (Lunch Peak)

4 PM – 7 PM (Dinner Peak)

This insight helps businesses optimize:

Staff scheduling

Kitchen operations

Marketing promotions during peak hours

Weekly Order Trends

Weekly trend analysis showed fluctuations in orders with the highest sales during week 48 (December), indicating strong seasonal demand.

Sales by Pizza Category

Sales distribution across pizza categories:

Classic – Highest contributor

Supreme – Second highest

Chicken and Veggie categories follow

This insight helps prioritize high-performing product categories.

Sales by Pizza Size

Customer preferences by size:

Large Pizza: 45.9% of total sales

Medium Pizza: 30.5%

Regular Pizza: 21.8%

Large pizzas dominate customer demand, suggesting bundle offers or promotions for large sizes.

Top Performing Pizzas

Based on revenue and orders:

Top performers include:

Thai Chicken Pizza

Barbecue Chicken Pizza

Classic Deluxe Pizza

Hawaiian Pizza

Pepperoni Pizza

These products drive a significant portion of total revenue.

Underperforming Pizzas

Lowest performing pizzas include:

Brie Carre Pizza

Green Garden Pizza

Spinach Supreme Pizza

Mediterranean Pizza

Spinach Pesto Pizza

These items may require:

Marketing promotions

Menu optimization

Possible removal from menu

6. Dashboard Features

The Tableau dashboard includes:

KPI summary cards

Hourly sales trend chart

Weekly order trend chart

Sales distribution by category and size

Top 5 best-selling pizzas

Bottom 5 worst-selling pizzas

This interactive dashboard enables stakeholders to monitor business performance effectively.

7. Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis:

1️⃣ Focus marketing campaigns on top-selling pizzas
2️⃣ Promote large pizza bundles due to high customer demand
3️⃣ Optimize staffing during peak hours (12–1 PM and 4–7 PM)
4️⃣ Reevaluate or promote underperforming pizzas to improve sales

8. Conclusion

The pizza sales analysis demonstrates how data-driven insights can improve decision-making in the food and retail industry. By leveraging SQL for data analysis and Tableau for visualization, businesses can better understand customer behavior, optimize product offerings, and enhance operational efficiency.
