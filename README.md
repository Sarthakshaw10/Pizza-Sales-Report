# Pizza-Sales-Report
Analyzed pizza sales data using SQL and Tableau to evaluate business performance and customer behavior. Calculated KPIs such as total revenue, average order value, and total orders, and built dashboards showing hourly trends, category sales, and top products, enabling data-driven insights for sales strategy and menu optimization.
Introduction

This project focuses on analyzing pizza sales data to understand business performance, customer purchasing behavior, and product demand. The analysis was conducted using SQL for data extraction and KPI calculations and Tableau for data visualization. The objective of the project is to generate meaningful insights that can support better decision-making in areas such as sales strategy, product optimization, and operational efficiency.

Tools & Technologies

The project utilizes MS SQL Server for managing and querying the dataset. SQL was used to perform data extraction, transformation, and business metric calculations. Tableau was used to build interactive dashboards and visualizations that highlight important trends and patterns in the data. Microsoft Excel was used for initial data preprocessing and validation.

Dataset Overview

The dataset consists of transactional pizza sales data containing information such as order identifiers, order date and time, pizza category, pizza size, pizza name, quantity sold, and price. The analysis includes more than 21,000 orders and approximately 49,000 pizzas sold, providing a comprehensive view of sales performance and customer preferences.

Key Performance Indicators

Several business metrics were calculated using SQL queries to evaluate the overall performance of the business. The analysis shows that the total revenue generated from pizza sales is $817.86K. The average order value is $38.3, representing the average amount spent per transaction. The total number of pizzas sold is approximately 49.6K, while the total number of orders placed is 21.4K. Based on these metrics, the average pizzas per order is calculated to be 2.32, indicating that customers often purchase more than one pizza per order.

Data Analysis and Insights

The analysis of hourly order patterns reveals that customer demand peaks during 12 PM to 1 PM, which corresponds to the lunch period, and again between 4 PM and 7 PM, which aligns with the evening dining hours. Understanding these peak periods can help businesses optimize staff scheduling, kitchen operations, and targeted marketing promotions.

The weekly sales trend shows variations in order volume throughout the year, with the highest number of orders occurring during week 48 in December, suggesting increased demand during the holiday season.

Sales distribution across pizza categories indicates that Classic pizzas generate the highest revenue, followed by Supreme pizzas, while Chicken and Veggie categories contribute a moderate share of total sales. This information helps identify which product categories drive the majority of revenue.

Customer preferences based on pizza size show that Large pizzas account for approximately 45.9% of total sales, followed by Medium pizzas at 30.5% and Regular pizzas at 21.8%. The dominance of large pizzas suggests that promotional offers or bundle deals centered around large sizes could further increase revenue.

The analysis of product performance highlights several pizzas that generate the highest revenue and order volume, including Thai Chicken Pizza, Barbecue Chicken Pizza, Classic Deluxe Pizza, Hawaiian Pizza, and Pepperoni Pizza. These items represent key revenue drivers for the business. In contrast, certain pizzas such as Brie Carre Pizza, Green Garden Pizza, Spinach Supreme Pizza, Mediterranean Pizza, and Spinach Pesto Pizza show lower sales performance and may require marketing promotion, menu repositioning, or potential removal from the menu.

Dashboard Overview

An interactive Tableau dashboard was created to visualize the results of the analysis. The dashboard includes key performance indicators, hourly sales trends, weekly order patterns, category-wise and size-wise sales distributions, and visual comparisons of the best and worst performing pizzas. This dashboard allows stakeholders to easily monitor business performance and identify trends in customer demand.

Business Insights

The insights derived from this analysis suggest that marketing campaigns should focus on promoting the highest performing pizzas to maximize revenue. The popularity of large pizzas indicates an opportunity to introduce bundle offers or promotional deals centered on larger sizes. Operational efficiency can also be improved by aligning staff scheduling with peak ordering hours. Additionally, underperforming products may require reevaluation through targeted marketing efforts or menu optimization strategies.

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how data-driven analysis can provide valuable insights into sales performance and customer behavior in the food industry. By leveraging SQL for analytical processing and Tableau for visualization, businesses can gain a deeper understanding of customer preferences, optimize their product offerings, and improve operational decision-making. The approach highlights the importance of combining data analysis with visual storytelling to support strategic business growth.
